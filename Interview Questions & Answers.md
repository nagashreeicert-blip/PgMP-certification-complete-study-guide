## üü¢ Beginner-Level Questions & Answers (10)

1. **What is a Project?**  
   A project is a temporary endeavor undertaken to create a unique product, service, or result.

2. **What is Program Management?**  
   Program management is managing **multiple related projects together** to achieve strategic business objectives.

3. **Who is a Stakeholder?**  
   A stakeholder is anyone who is affected by or can affect the outcome of a project or program.

4. **What is Project Scope?**  
   Scope defines all the work required to deliver a project‚Äôs objectives.

5. **What is a Deliverable?**  
   A deliverable is a tangible or intangible output produced by a project or program.

6. **What is Work Breakdown Structure (WBS)?**  
   WBS is a hierarchical decomposition of project deliverables into smaller, manageable components.

7. **What is Risk in a project?**  
   Risk is an uncertain event that can have a positive or negative impact on project objectives.

8. **What is the difference between Project and Operations?**  
   - Projects are temporary with a specific goal.  
   - Operations are ongoing and repetitive activities.

9. **Name the 5 Project Management Process Groups.**  
   Initiating, Planning, Executing, Monitoring & Controlling, Closing.

10. **What is a Milestone in project management?**  
    A milestone is a significant point or event in a project timeline.

## üü° Intermediate-Level Questions & Answers (10)

1. **What is a Program Charter?**  
   A document that formally authorizes a program, defines its objectives, scope, stakeholders, and governance.

2. **What is a RAID Log?**  
   A tool to track **Risks, Assumptions, Issues, and Dependencies** in a program/project.

3. **What is the Critical Path Method (CPM)?**  
   CPM identifies the sequence of activities that determines the project duration.

4. **What is a Change Request?**  
   A formal proposal to modify any aspect of a project or program.

5. **Explain Resource Leveling.**  
   Adjusting the project schedule to resolve resource over-allocation without changing the project scope.

6. **What is a Baseline?**  
   A reference point for scope, schedule, or cost against which performance is measured.

7. **What is Earned Value (EV)?**  
   A metric that measures the value of work actually completed compared to planned work.

8. **What is a Kickoff Meeting?**  
   A meeting to officially start the project or program and align all stakeholders.

9. **What is a Risk Register?**  
   A document listing all identified risks, their assessment, and planned responses.

10. **Difference between Issue and Risk?**  
    - Issue: Current problem affecting the project.  
    - Risk: Potential future event that may impact the project.

## üî¥ Advanced-Level Questions & Answers (10)

1. **What are CPI and SPI in Earned Value Management?**  
   - CPI (Cost Performance Index) = EV / AC  
   - SPI (Schedule Performance Index) = EV / PV  

2. **What is Quantitative Risk Analysis?**  
   Using numerical methods to analyze the impact and probability of risks on project objectives.

3. **What is a RACI Chart?**  
   A responsibility assignment matrix that defines **Responsible, Accountable, Consulted, and Informed** roles.

4. **Explain Rolling Wave Planning.**  
   Planning technique where near-term work is planned in detail and future work is planned at a higher level.

5. **What is Hybrid Project Management?**  
   Combines **Agile and Waterfall** methods to leverage benefits of both approaches.

6. **What is Cost of Quality (COQ)?**  
   Total cost of ensuring quality including prevention, appraisal, and failure costs.

7. **What is a Change Control Board (CCB)?**  
   A group of stakeholders responsible for reviewing, approving, or rejecting change requests.

8. **Explain Monte Carlo Simulation in project/program management.**  
   A statistical technique to estimate probable outcomes by simulating project scenarios multiple times.

9. **What is Procurement Management in programs?**  
   Process of acquiring goods and services from external suppliers while managing contracts and compliance.

10. **What is a Program Management Plan?**  
    A comprehensive plan describing how a program will be executed, monitored, and controlled, including governance, roadmap, resource allocation, and benefits realization.
